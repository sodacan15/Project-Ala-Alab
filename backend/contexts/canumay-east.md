## Ecological Records

#### Eleven Creeks — Ecological History of Canumay East
- **Date of observation:** 1960–2026 (historical range)
- **Date of submission:** 2026-06-29
- **Source type:** [ORAL] + [FIELD] + [SECONDARY]
- **Data flow direction:** Bottom-up
- **Contributor:** Community elder (Sitio Libis); barangay worker; field documentarian
- **Content:** At least eleven creeks historically ran through what is now Canumay East. Names include Sapang Pangitlogan-Bakaw (mangrove nesting ground), Sapang Bangka-bangka (boat-navigable depth), and Sapang Bato. NLEX construction (October 2, 1966, 512th Engineering Construction Battalion) severed and buried several creek paths. None of the eleven are intact today. Water from former creek beds still surfaces during heavy rain events through subsurface drainage, causing localized flooding in Sitio Libis and nearby areas.
- **Significance:** Ecological / Historical / Disaster
- **Linked entries:** See Flood Geometry Documentation (Active Threads); Sapang Pangitlogan-Bakaw (below)

#### Kanumai Tree — Barangay Namesake
- **Date of observation:** 2026-06-15 (field walk)
- **Date of submission:** 2026-06-29
- **Source type:** [FIELD] + [ORAL]
- **Data flow direction:** Bottom-up
- **Contributor:** Field documentarian; long-term resident testimony
- **Content:** The Kanumai tree (Canarium ovatum, pili) historically gave the barangay its name — Canumay is derived from Kanumai. Three field walks conducted in June 2026 found no living specimen in the barangay. Elder oral testimony confirms the tree was present in living memory but has since been lost to development and land-use change. The official Barangay Profile still lists it as a current landmark without noting its absence.
- **Significance:** Ecological / Historical
- **Linked entries:** Kanumai Tree Status (Active Threads); Barangay Profile (Official Records)

- **Date of observation:** Pre-1966 (historical); corroborated 2026
- **Date of submission:** 2026-06-29
- **Source type:** [ORAL] + [SECONDARY]
- **Data flow direction:** Bottom-up
- **Contributor:** Community elder (Sitio Libis); creek name etymology analysis
- **Content:** Creek name Sapang Pangitlogan-Bakaw translates to "mangrove nesting ground." The name indicates the creek supported mangrove vegetation and bird nesting prior to NLEX construction. ## Community & Oral History

#### Flood Memory — Sitio Libis Residents
- **Date of observation:** 2024–2026 (recurring)
- **Date of submission:** 2026-06-29
- **Source type:** [ORAL]
- **Data flow direction:** Bottom-up
- **Contributor:** Sitio Libis residents (multiple, names withheld on request)
- **Content:** Residents report that Sitio Libis experiences flooding from below — water rises through low-lying ground before or concurrent with surface rainfall. Several residents describe the depression in the empty lot near Victoria Village as the first point where floodwater pools, then spreads outward. This pattern is consistent with subsurface drainage along a former creek bed, not overland flow from rain alone.
- **Significance:** Disaster / Health / Community-relevant
- **Linked entries:** Flood Geometry Documentation (Active Threads); Eleven Creeks (Ecological Records)

- **Source type:** [ORAL]
- **Data flow direction:** Bottom-up

## Infrastructure & Policy Friction

#### Official Landmark vs. Ground Truth — Kanumai Tree
- **Official position:** Barangay Profile of Canumay East (2022) identifies the Kanumai tree as the barangay's defining landmark. The barangay name derives from this tree.
- **Ground truth:** Three field walks (June 2026) found no living Kanumai tree in the barangay. Long-term resident oral testimony confirms the tree is no longer present.
- **Contradiction type:** Active official record vs. observed ecological reality
- **Status:** Unresolved — flagged for human review. Recommended action: official update to Barangay Profile with erratum noting tree loss and estimated date range.
- **Implication:** Any LGU document citing the Kanumai tree as an existing landmark is citing an extinct ecological feature. The historical significance is real and should be preserved; the present-tense framing is inaccurate and should be corrected.

#### Flood Risk Data — Absent from Formal LGU Records
- **Official position:** No formal flood risk mapping found for Canumay East at barangay level. Valenzuela City ecological reports do not include Sitio Libis subsurface drainage patterns.
- **Ground truth:** Oral testimony and field observation document recurring below-surface flooding tied to former creek bed geometry. The pattern is consistent and recurring across multiple rainy seasons.
- **Contradiction type:** Community-observed risk vs. absent institutional record
- **Status:** Unresolved — flagged for LGU escalation.
- **Implication:** Emergency response plans for Sitio Libis flood events are built without knowledge of subsurface drainage geometry. Responders entering the area during a typhoon event would not know which sitios flood first or where water pools — information held only in oral memory.
